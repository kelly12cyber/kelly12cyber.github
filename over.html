OVERVIEW OF GHANA

Discover Ghana Tourism Website

Tourism

Tourism is the activity of people travelling from one place to another for leisure, education, business, or cultural purposes. It includes visiting new destinations to experience different culture, traditions, natural attractions, and historical sites.

Tourism contributes greatly to the economy of many countries. It creates jobs, supports local businesses, and helps in the development of infrastructure such as hotels, roads, and transport services. In Ghana, tourism is an important sector because it showcases the country's rich history, wildlife, festivals, and natural beauty. Tourist sites such as castles, national parks, lakes, and cultural festivals attract visitors from around the world and help preserve Ghana's heritage for future generations.

About the Destination

Ghana is known as the 'Gateway to Africa'. It was the first African country south of the Sahara to gain independence in 1957. It is bordered by Burkina Faso, Togo, and the Atlantic Ocean. The country has diverse ethnic groups, colourful traditions, and delicious local foods such as jollof, rice, fufu, and banku. English is the official language, making it easy for tourists to communicate.
